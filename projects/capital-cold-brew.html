Temple Coffee Roasters · 2024–2026

Capital Cold Brew: Seasonal Packaging Evolution

Role

Sole designer for the label, merchandise, and packaging. Photography direction shared with Temple's creative director and social media manager.

Timeline

2024 – 2026, annual release

Deliverables

Bag label design, print files, seasonal merchandise, photoshoot direction

Format

Small-format retail bag label as the anchor for the full release

[ HERO IMAGE, MOST RECENT PACKAGING ]

Problem

Capital Cold Brew, or CCB, is Temple's annual summer release: a blend built specifically for brewing cold brew at home. The name plays on Sacramento's identity as California's capital, and California is where the feeling of summer lives most vividly in the popular imagination: sun, waves, surf. The job each year is bottling that feeling and putting it on a coffee bag.

The design challenge starts with format. Temple's retail bag label is small, and translating something as broad as "California summer" into a concept that fits on it, while still feeling distinct from the year before, is the constraint every release has to solve first.

Process

Every release starts with the label. Once that concept is set, everything else builds from it: merchandise, in-café signage, and the direction for that year's product photography, developed together with Temple's creative director and social media manager using the label as our shared reference point.

What stays consistent year to year is the throughline: sun and waves, the visual shorthand that ties CCB back to California summer no matter how the concept evolves. What changes is how literally or abstractly that idea gets rendered.

Constraints

The label is small. Every concept has to read as unmistakably summer at that scale before it extends anywhere else.

Result

Like Wonderblend, CCB is a release customers look forward to each year, especially when it comes with merchandise. The 2025 release paired the label with a glass mug and a sticker, extending the sun-and-waves concept into physical goods people keep using well past the season.
